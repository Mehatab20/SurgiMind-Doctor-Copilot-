TITLE: ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction (STEMI) – Perioperative Surgical Management Protocol

CATEGORY: Cardiology | Interventional Cardiology | Emergency | Cardiac Surgery

OVERVIEW:
STEMI is an acute coronary syndrome caused by complete occlusion of a coronary artery resulting in full-thickness myocardial infarction. It represents the highest-priority cardiac emergency requiring reperfusion within 90 minutes of first medical contact (door-to-balloon time). Any non-cardiac surgical intervention during the acute phase of STEMI carries profound risk of haemodynamic collapse, arrhythmia, and perioperative death.

SURGICAL INDICATIONS:
- Primary percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI): first-line reperfusion for STEMI (Door-to-Balloon < 90 min)
- Emergency CABG: failed PCI, left main or multi-vessel disease not amenable to PCI, mechanical complications
- Surgical repair of mechanical complications: ventricular septal defect (VSD), free wall rupture, acute mitral regurgitation from papillary muscle rupture
- Intra-aortic balloon pump (IABP) placement for cardiogenic shock as bridge to revascularisation
- Extracorporeal membrane oxygenation (ECMO) / Impella insertion for refractory cardiogenic shock
- Emergency aortic surgery if STEMI caused by type A aortic dissection extending to coronary ostia

CONTRAINDICATIONS FOR ELECTIVE SURGERY:
- Any elective non-cardiac surgery within 14 days of STEMI (Class III, ACC/AHA Guidelines)
- Drug-eluting stent placement within 12 months: antiplatelet therapy must not be stopped (risk of in-stent thrombosis)
- Bare metal stent placement within 4-6 weeks: minimum antiplatelet duration before elective surgery
- Active cardiogenic shock requiring vasopressor or mechanical circulatory support
- Ejection fraction < 30% post-STEMI without cardiac optimisation
- Active DAPT (dual antiplatelet therapy) on prasugrel or ticagrelor: haemorrhagic surgical risk
- Unresolved ventricular arrhythmias post-STEMI (sustained VT/VF)
- Symptomatic heart failure / pulmonary oedema not controlled medically

PRE-OPERATIVE CHECKLIST:
1. STAT 12-lead ECG at presentation: identify ST elevation ≥ 1 mm in ≥ 2 contiguous leads (> 2 mm V1-V4)
2. Immediate cardiac catheterisation lab activation: bypass ED if STEMI confirmed
3. Serial troponin I/T: at presentation, 3h, 6h to track infarct size and peak
4. Echocardiography (transthoracic): regional wall motion abnormalities, EF, mechanical complications
5. Full blood count: pre-procedure baseline, post-procedure haematocrit check
6. Coagulation panel: PTT, ACT monitoring during PCI heparinisation
7. Electrolytes: K+ > 4.0, Mg2+ > 0.8 mEq/L (antiarrhythmic targets pre-cardioversion/surgery)
8. Renal function: creatinine and eGFR before contrast administration (contrast nephropathy risk)
9. Glucose management: target 140-180 mg/dL perioperatively (hyperglycaemia worsens ischaemic injury)
10. Antiplatelet loading: aspirin 300 mg + P2Y12 inhibitor (ticagrelor 180 mg or clopidogrel 600 mg)
11. Anticoagulation: UFH 60-70 units/kg IV bolus at cath lab or bivalirudin as alternative
12. Opioid analgesia: IV morphine or fentanyl for pain control (reduce sympathetic activation)
13. Beta-blockade: oral metoprolol if no contraindication (bradycardia, hypotension, bronchospasm)
14. Statin loading: high-intensity statin (atorvastatin 80 mg) immediately
15. ICU/HDU admission post-intervention: telemetry for minimum 24 hours
16. If CABG planned post-failed PCI: hold clopidogrel 5 days, prasugrel/ticagrelor 7 days pre-surgery

RED FLAGS / ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Active STEMI with cardiogenic shock: mortality exceeds 40% even with revascularisation
- Mechanical complications (acute VSD, free wall rupture): surgical mortality > 25%, but surgery is only option
- Troponin elevation > 50x upper limit of normal with persistent ST changes: large territory infarct, higher arrest risk intraoperatively
- Systolic BP < 80 mmHg unresponsive to 500 mL fluid challenge: cardiogenic shock, MCS required before surgical positioning
- New LBBB with chest pain in STEMI-equivalent territory: treat as STEMI regardless of ECG ambiguity
- Bilateral pulmonary oedema on chest X-ray with SpO2 < 90% on high-flow O2: respiratory failure from LV dysfunction
- Drug-eluting stent within 12 months + cessation of antiplatelet therapy: stent thrombosis mortality risk 20-40%
- Systemic thrombolysis administered within 24 hours: absolute contraindication to most surgical haemostasis
